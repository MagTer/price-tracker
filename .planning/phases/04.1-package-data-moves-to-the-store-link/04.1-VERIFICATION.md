### Gaps Summary

**None.** Every code-level must-have is verified against the codebase, not against SUMMARY claims. I independently re-ran the suite in both DB states, ran the named behavioral tests, drove the pure JS sort in node, and confirmed the static gate can fail.

Two things I specifically tried to falsify and could not:

1. **"One definition" of unit price** — I grepped for a second copy of the g→kg/ml→liter arithmetic and for consumers re-deriving `price/qty` inline. There is no second copy in Python, and every consumer (`service`, `admin`, the ORM hybrid, MCP) delegates to `pricing.unit_price_py`/`unit_price_expr`. The JS mirror in admin.html is unavoidable (the browser needs it) and is pinned by a drift gate.
2. **"needs amount" sorting as free** — the single most dangerous regression this phase could ship (a quantity-less link winning "cheapest"). Closed at both layers independently: Postgres `NULLIF` + NULLS-last on the server, and an explicit missing-last comparator in the client, which I executed.

The three known open items (D-15 deployed reset, plan 08 Task 4 operator checkpoint, AC#4 real-page scrape) are **operator work, not code gaps** — they are correctly scoped out of the phase's own deliverable, and the artifact the phase DID owe for D-15 (the runbook) exists and is accurate.
