<objective>
Rewire the domain's two engines — the service and the scheduler — onto the computed unit price, and make
every scrape write-path record the page's quantity reading.

Purpose: this is where the phase's most dangerous failure hides. The watch evaluation is in
`PriceScheduler._check_alerts()`, NOT in `notifier.py` (which only renders HTML). Left untouched, the
scheduler keeps referencing the dropped `unit_price_sek` column and DIES UNATTENDED at 06:00 — and the
`TypeError` is swallowed by the per-product `except Exception` at `scheduler.py:157`, so it fails SILENTLY,
counted as `checks_failed`. It must be rewired here, with an explicit NULL guard.

Output: `service.py` with package data on the link, a reshaped `get_price_history`, a new
`get_links_for_product`, and both service-side scrape paths applying D-07; `scheduler.py` with the
back-derivation hack deleted and the NULL-quantity guard in place; both test suites green.
</objective>
